Home

Remi was technically born in a hospital in Sellserville, PA but her childhood home is located in an even lesser known town called West Rockhill. She lived in the same house, built in the 1800's, until she left for New York City at age 18. She has lived many places over the years, but no matter where she lives, she loves entertaining.

This is Remi's childhood home--a house that is missed every single day. The house was built room by room with stones from the surrounding land by John Fries, the leader of a local tax rebellion.

Remi's first NYC home. It was a block away from Washington Square park and once housed the likes of Jack Kerouac, which is why she picked this at her dorm. She does not miss living here one bit.

Remi moved out of dorm life and lived in many places all over Brooklyn, mostly Williamsburg. Her favorite apartment was down the street from the factory that makes most of the fortune cookies people eat.

When she got priced out of Brooklyn, Remi moved to Astoria, Queens. Astoria was the land of great ethnic food--the thing she misses most since leaving NYC. This apartment saw a number of crazy parties.

Remi was finally priced out of New York City all together and decided to move closer to home. Her favorite things about Philadelphia lviing are her dishwasher, outdoor patio and being closer to family. Also the fact that the extra space made getting a dog possible.
